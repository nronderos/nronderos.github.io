Nicolás Ronderos, PhD

Curriculum Vitae

Google Scholar

I am a professor with a strong academic interest in quantitative methods in economics, with particular emphasis on time series analysis, applied macroeconometrics, and financial econometrics. My research focuses on the study of dynamic economic processes using advanced and innovative econometric methodologies, with a sustained interest in forecasting and participation in international forecasting competitions as a rigorous benchmark for model performance.

A central line of my work examines the macroeconomic effects of informal sectors on economic outcomes, including growth, labor markets, health economics and institutional quality. I have designed modified instrumental variable methods aimed at attenuating endogeneity and weak-instrument problems in large samples. I have also conducted research on contagion in financial markets, portfolio theory, and volatility modeling, combining structural, spectral, and multivariate approaches to analyze risk transmission and asset dynamics. My research has resulted in peer-reviewed publications on topics such as applied macroeconomic dynamics, econometric methods and health economics as well as the development of certified econometric software tools. Complementing this academic work, I have participated in policy-oriented consulting projects with government institutions, where methodological rigor must align with real-world decision-making.

Outside academia, I maintain an active engagement in endurance sports—particularly amateur competitive cycling—, which contribute to a balanced approach to academic productivity, social life, and physical and mental well-being.
